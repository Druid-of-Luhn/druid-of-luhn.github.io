Special Abilities

Track

The Knight may attempt to track their opponents or prey animals across country (pg. 63). The Knight can follow trails up to three days old. Roll 1d20 ≤ Perception to track quarry for one day before making another roll. An additional roll is required every time the trail crosses from one type of terrain to another (e.g. from woodlands to hills), and also if the quarry fords a river. After three days the trail grows obscure and 1d4 is added to the 1d20 die roll for each day thereafter, plus an extra 1d6 for each snowfall or heavy storm that occurs.

Armour Expert

The Knight is the only character who can wear a suit of Plate Armour without any penalties to Attack and Defence. The Knight never suffers any Attack or Defence penalties for wearing armour.

Ride Warhorse

All characters may ride a horse, but only Knights, Barbarians and some Warlocks have had the long years of special training required to ride a warhorse (pg. 245).

Character History

Brieuc is a holy relic hunter, although he has not had much success yet. Knightly activities such as being gallant, noble, devout and participating in tourneys keep him from actually going out and searching for holy relics. A recent dry spell on the tourney ground has left Brieuc with an empty purse and no horse. He must now rely on his sacred mission to build up his personal wealth and fame, in order to regain the respect of his fellow knights and the favour of the ladies of Chaubrette.

Having recently crossed the Glaive into Ellesland, Brieuc is now a knight errant in search of glory. His righteous quest is to return to Chaubrette with holy relics freed from misty barrows, deep caverns and dark forests.

Brieuc is fluent in Beaulangue and Elleslandic, and even knows a little Bacchile and Old Mercanian, and he is literate in all of them, the result of a strict education provided by his father. Spurred on by the tales of wandering knights errant, beset by foes in far-off lands, Brieuc was inspired to actually listen to his lessons on languages, which would help him discover the locations of the holy relics he would seek out once he grew up. His tutor failed to educate him in anything else however, be it history, geography, mathematics or medicine, as Brieuc's mind was too busy imagining glorious charges and heroic duels.
